RANKED #2

Freebird (FlexSeries)

★★★★☆ 8.5/10

Freebird has massive brand awareness and their FlexSeries delivers a very decent, close shave. However, they lost the #1 spot due to their billing model. Many customers complain about being quietly enrolled into a recurring blade subscription during checkout. If you don't mind paying $20+ every month for replacement blades and dealing with automated customer service bots to cancel, it's a solid machine.

RANKED #3

Skull Shaver (Pitbull Series)

★★★★☆ 8.2/10

The pioneer of the palm-held design. The Skull Shaver feels great in the hand and features a patented 4-blade rotary system. The main issue dragging its score down is durability. A vast repository of reviews indicates that the internal lithium batteries and motors frequently die just after the 1-year warranty expires, forcing you to buy a completely new unit.

RANKED #4

Philips Norelco

★★★☆☆ 7.8/10

A massive household name, the Philips Norelco series is highly reliable and easy to clean. However, these shavers are primarily designed for the face, not the posterior curves of the skull. The rigid neck doesn't pivot enough to perfectly hug the back of the head, often requiring 10-15 repetitive passes which severely irritates the scalp.

RANKED #5

Manscaped (The Dome Shaver)

★★★☆☆ 7.5/10

Manscaped's entry into the head-shaving market is undeniably sleek and premium-looking. Unfortunately, testers noted that the blades are overly aggressive, with many reporting a "scratching" sensation when shaving dry. Furthermore, replacing the head independently is notoriously difficult without getting locked into their broader ecosystem.
